F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How long does the process take?

    Contracts are generally signed within 1-2 weeks. When an offer is made, there must be an acceptance or a counter offer made within 24 to 48 hours.

  • How is the listing price set?

    A Comparative Market Analysis (CMA) is run that will show the listing price of similar houses in the area as well as the prices at which the houses actually sold. Next, I factor in how quickly houses are selling in your area, interest rates, the strength of the school system, and finally whether it is a buyer's or seller's market. I'll then recommend a price at which to list your house to meet your goals in the local market.

  • How will I get the best price for my home?

    I am dedicated to the effective marketing of my client's homes by every available medium. We'll work closely together to assure that everything possible is done to get the highest price, in the time frame you need. I'll personally walk through your home with you and advise you of what you can do to properly stage your home most effectively. As your agent, I will negotiate furiously on your behalf throughout the entire process to ensure that your best interests are protected.

  • How do I prepare my house for sale?

    Before putting your property on the market, we should make it look as appealing as possible for potential buyers. How does it look from the outside? Can we improve the lawn/garden area? How is the interior vibe? Does it feel homely and welcoming? Before the first buyer walks in your door, we'll set the stage. We want to engage the buyer's senses. Lighting is critical. We'll draw back curtains, open blinds, change light bulbs and add lighting where needed to welcome the buyer.

  • What mortgage rates do I qualify for?

    We use the FICO score, which is based on a lifetime of credit history, to determine affordability. FICO scores are used for more than just determining whether or not you qualify for a mortgage. Higher scores indicate you are a better credit risk, and thus may qualify for a better mortgage rate.

  • How does Weldon Enterprises work?

    To finalize the sale of the home a neutral, third party (the Weldon Enterprises holder, a.k.a. Weldon Enterprises agent) is engaged to assure the transaction will close properly and on time. The Weldon Enterprises holder insures that all terms and conditions of the seller's and buyer's agreement are met prior to the sale being finalized, including receiving funds and documents, completing required forms, and obtaining the release documents for any loans or liens that have been paid off with the transaction, assuring you clear title to your property before the purchase price is fully paid. Upon completion of all instructions of the Weldon Enterprises, closing can take place. All outstanding payments and fees are collected and paid at this time (covering expenses such as title insurance, inspections, real estate commissions). Title to the property is then transferred to the seller and appropriate title insurance is issued as outlined in the Weldon Enterprises instructions.
